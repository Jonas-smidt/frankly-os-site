04 / Production

AI prompt phrasing

External AI tools (Flow, Veo, Nano Banana…) don’t know our internal brand language. Never write “Frankly pink” or “the ribbon” in a prompt — use these physical descriptions instead. Click to copy.

Prompt structure

Image prompts follow the six-block structure: Subject → Composition → Action → Location → Style & Mood → Tech Specs (aspect ratio belongs in Tech Specs, never mid-prompt). Minimal prompt + one targeted nudge beats over-specification.

05 / Production

Hard rules

The four absolutes. Everything else is judgment; these are not.

RULE_01 / RIBBON

Ribbon

Never a figure-eight / infinity shape (reads as the breast-cancer awareness symbol). The ribbon flows in from outside one frame edge and exits beyond another — no visible ends inside the frame. It orbits products; it never wraps tightly around them. No ribbons in real-life lifestyle scenes.

RULE_02 / PINK

Pink

Clear, warm, sweet-leaning — never dusty, muted or desaturated in imagery. In scenes, pink appears as substantial anchor objects (a full wall, a cargo bike), not scattered small props.

RULE_03 / VOICE

Voice

Empowerment and confidence, never fear or anxiety. Plain language. “Et godt liv skal ikke leves forsigtigt.” LinkedIn posts and pitch decks are always in English.

RULE_04 / LIGHT

Light & film

Describe skies concretely and positively (structured cumulus, pink-peach lower edges, sun fully out of frame) — the phrase “golden hour” produces fake orange glow. Kodak Portra 400 grain, warm amber tones, brown shadows — never cool or blue.

06 / Quick answers

Can I…?

The most common questions, answered straight. Not covered here? Ask the Brand Assistant in Claude — or Jonas.

Can I use the logo in green (or any other color)?

No. The wordmark is used in Off Black #111111. The documented palette is the complete approved set — green, blue, red etc. are not part of the system. Need a white/negative version for dark backgrounds? Ask Jonas.

Can the ribbon form a figure-eight?

No — hard rule. A figure-eight/infinity reads as the breast-cancer awareness symbol. The ribbon flows in from one frame edge and out beyond another, with no visible ends.

Can I write a LinkedIn post in Danish?

No. Frankly LinkedIn posts are always in English, regardless of partner or market context. Same goes for pitch decks.

Can I use “golden hour” in an AI image prompt?

Avoid it. The phrase produces fake orange glow. Describe the sky concretely instead: structured cumulus, pink-peach lower edges, sun fully out of frame.

Can I put a ribbon in a lifestyle photo?

No. Ribbons belong to the 3D/sculptural visual route only — never in real-life scenes.

Can I use a muted/dusty pink in imagery?

No. In imagery, pink is always clear, warm and sweet-leaning. (Dirty Pink #F3DEE4 exists as a tertiary UI/graphic color only.)

Can I use Almarena for body text?

No. Almarena Neue Display is for headlines and hero copy only. Body copy and UI run Switzer.

Can I write “Frankly pink” in a Flow/Veo prompt?

No. External tools don’t know our brand language. Use physical descriptions + hex: “soft warm pink, hex #FFD3E3, never dusty”.

Can I use fear-framing (“don’t risk losing…”) in copy?

Never. The voice is empowerment and confidence. Reframe to what the customer gains: living freely, fully covered.

Can I quote our pitch numbers in a post or deck?

Only locked, approved numbers — and never invent or extrapolate. If you’re unsure whether a number is approved, ask Jonas, who routes to Alexander/Nicolai.
